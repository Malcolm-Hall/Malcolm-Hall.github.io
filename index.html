About Me

I am a recent Natural Science graduate with a 1st class honours degree in Physics and Mathematics from the University of Durham. I have had a personal interest in programming throughout my degree and have self-studied key programming concepts such as Object Oriented Programming (OOP), design patterns, data structures, algorithms and unit testing, among many other topics.

Throughout my degree I have used Python for automating data collection, analysis, and visualisation. During a summer studentship role in 2019 I gained experience working in a team of 2 on a programming project in Python, which I believe kick-started my passion for programming. C++ was a major component of my masters dissertation where I extended the program Herwig 7 with physics related to my dissertation, which taught me many new concepts and solidified my love for programming.

I am a quick learner of new concepts in programming, and I am always eager to learn new skills, technologies, and programming languages. I am also a great problem-solver with an analytical mind and enjoy the challenges associated with developing software.
